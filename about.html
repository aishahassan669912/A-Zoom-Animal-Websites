About Our Wildlife Sanctuary

Our Story

Founded in 2010, A-Zoom Wildlife Sanctuary began as a small rescue center for injured animals. Today, we've grown into one of Kenya's premier conservation facilities, spanning over 200 acres of protected habitat. Our journey started with just three dedicated staff members caring for orphaned elephants, and has since expanded to a team of 50+ wildlife experts protecting more than 100 species.

What makes us unique is our community-based approach. We work hand-in-hand with local Maasai communities to develop sustainable conservation solutions that benefit both wildlife and people. Through eco-tourism and education programs, we've created economic opportunities that make conservation profitable for local residents.

Our Mission

We exist to protect Africa's magnificent wildlife while creating meaningful connections between humans and nature. Our three core pillars are: conservation (protecting endangered species), education (teaching the next generation), and sustainability (developing eco-friendly solutions).

Each year, we rehabilitate over 200 injured animals, conduct 50+ school outreach programs, and train 30 local wildlife rangers. Our research initiatives have contributed to 15 published scientific papers on African wildlife behavior and habitat preservation techniques.
